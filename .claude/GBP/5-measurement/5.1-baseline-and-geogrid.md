# Step 5.1 — Baseline, Geo-Grid Scans, and Attribution
> Human process, not an AI prompt. Run the baseline capture on day 1 of onboarding, before any optimization touches the profile. Without a baseline, you can't prove the work did anything — and an unprovable result is how a client cancels in month 3.

---

## Baseline capture (day 1, before any edit)

```
[ ] Geo-grid scan: a snapshot of current Map Pack visibility across a grid of
    points around the business, for the 3-5 keywords that matter most
    ("plumber", "emergency plumber", "drain cleaning" — whatever fits the niche)
[ ] GBP Insights export: calls, direction requests, website clicks, photo views
    for the last 3 months
[ ] Full screenshot of the current profile (every field, as-is)
[ ] Current review count, current average rating, date of the most recent review
[ ] Current Map Pack position for the primary keyword (search incognito, not
    logged in — logged-in search personalizes results)
```

Save all of this. It's the "before" half of every before/after you'll show the client later.

---

## Attribution setup

```
[ ] Add a UTM parameter to the website link inside the GBP profile:
    ?utm_source=google&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=gbp
    This separates GBP-driven traffic from generic organic traffic in the
    client's GA4 — without it, you can't isolate what your work is actually
    driving.
[ ] If the client uses call tracking: the tracking number goes in as the
    PRIMARY phone number, the real number as an additional number — in that
    order. Getting the order backwards creates a NAP inconsistency (a
    confirmed ranking factor), so check this carefully, not as an afterthought.
[ ] Export GBP Insights on the same day every month — comparing month-to-month
    numbers pulled on different days of the month introduces noise that looks
    like a trend when it isn't.
```

---

## Geo-grid scanning cadence

- Run monthly, always on the same day of the month (removes seasonal/weekly noise from the comparison)
- After any significant change (new primary category, a push for new reviews): run an extra scan two weeks later specifically to measure that change's impact
- The before/after heatmap is the single most persuasive thing you can show a client — visual, no jargon required to understand it

## Tools (confirmed pricing as of research date — verify current pricing before quoting)

| Tool | Price | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| GMB Radar | Free (300 credits/mo) | Best free starting point, white-label included |
| BrightLocal Local Search Grid | ~$39/mo (225 points included) | Full suite (citations + reviews + grid) once you have 3+ clients |
| Local Falcon | Credit-based | Larger grids (21x21) for big metro service areas |

**Recommendation:** start with GMB Radar (free) for the first 1-2 clients. Move to BrightLocal once volume justifies the cost — its white-label reports are a real upgrade for client-facing polish, not just a nice-to-have.

## What the monthly report leads with (see `../3-monthly-management/3.3-monthly-report-prompt.md`)

The business outcome first (calls, direction requests), the geo-grid heatmap second, the technical detail last if at all. A small business owner doesn't need to know what a "discovery impression" is, and no report should assume they do.
