# Step 0.1 — Client Intake & Due Diligence Checklist
> Human checklist, not an AI prompt. Run before accepting a GBP management client — not after. Confirmed: Sterling Sky (sterlingsky.ca/top-reasons-google-my-business-suspended-your-listing/), the most cited local-SEO agency on GBP suspensions.

---

## Why this runs before onboarding, not during

A suspended profile isn't just a lost client — if it happens because of something you did (or something the client's account already had wrong when you took it over), it can flag your agency account and cascade-suspend every other client you manage at the same time. This checklist exists to catch problems that belong to the client's existing setup before they become your problem.

---

## Business legitimacy

```
[ ] Address is real and receives mail (PO box or virtual/coworking office = high suspension risk)
[ ] Business has an actual physical presence at that address, or is correctly set up as a
    Service-Area Business with the address hidden (see 2-setup checklist) — Google's own
    guidance and Sterling Sky both confirm "no physical presence at the listed address" is
    a real, documented suspension trigger, not a myth
[ ] Client can produce: business registration, a utility or tax bill at the address, photos
    of the physical location with signage (if applicable)
```

## Existing profile audit (before you touch anything)

```
[ ] Does the current profile name already have keyword stuffing?
    ("ABC Plumbing — Best Emergency Plumber Tampa 24/7" instead of "ABC Plumbing")
    Fix this FIRST, in isolation, before any other optimization — don't bundle it with
    other changes (see 4-risk-protocol.md on batching edits)
[ ] Are there duplicate listings for this business anywhere? (same name/category/address
    appearing twice) Resolve before Google's own spam detection resolves it for you
[ ] Who currently has access to the profile? Remove old agencies and inactive users —
    a restricted third party with lingering access is a documented suspension risk
[ ] Is the business in a high-scrutiny category? (locksmiths, garage door services,
    addiction treatment/rehab are the categories Sterling Sky specifically flags as
    under heavier Google review) Doesn't mean don't take the client — means budget
    extra care and slower, more spaced-out edits
```

## Niche fit (informs pricing and effort, not a gate)

```
[ ] Does the niche have urgent search intent? ("emergency plumber near me" = someone
    calling now, not researching)
[ ] Is the average job ticket high enough that one new client covers months of your fee?
[ ] Does the owner have no time/interest in marketing themselves? (if they're already
    doing this well, there's less obvious value to add)
```

---

## Access setup (do this correctly from day one)

```
[ ] Your agency account is added as MANAGER — never accept or request Owner
[ ] Client keeps primary ownership on an account tied to their own business domain,
    not a personal Gmail
[ ] Confirm the client understands they can revoke your access at any time — this is
    a trust signal worth saying out loud, not just a technical fact
```

---

## Output of this step

A go/no-go decision, plus a short list of pre-existing issues to fix first (name stuffing, duplicates, stale access) before starting the setup checklist in `2-setup/2.1-onboarding-setup-checklist.md`. If the business fails the "legitimacy" section, do not proceed — that's not a fixable-later problem, it's a suspension waiting to happen with your name on the account.
