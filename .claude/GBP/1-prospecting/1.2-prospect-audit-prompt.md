# Step 1.2 — Prospect Audit & Cold Email Draft
> AI prompt. Run per prospect, after manually pulling their public profile data (Google Maps, incognito). Output: an opportunity score and a draft cold email. A human sends the email — never automate the send (see root README rule and `../4-risk-protocol.md`).

---

## Manual step first (5 minutes, do this yourself, not with AI)

1. Open Google Maps in incognito mode
2. Search `[niche] [city]`
3. Skip the Map Pack top 3 — they already rank, not your prospect
4. Open 3-5 businesses ranking positions 4-10, or not appearing at all
5. For each, note: review count, star rating, date of last review, date of last Google Post, photo count, whether a category looks generic ("Contractor" instead of "Emergency Plumber"), whether their site's NAP matches their GBP NAP

---

## Scoring (do this arithmetic yourself before handing to Claude — it's simple counting, not worth an AI call)

One point each:
```
[ ] Fewer than 20 reviews
[ ] Reviews with no owner response
[ ] Last review older than 30 days
[ ] Rating below 4.0
[ ] Fewer than 10 photos
[ ] No cover photo, or a generic/stock one
[ ] No photo uploads in the last 60 days
[ ] No Google Posts in the last 30 days
[ ] NAP on their own site doesn't match their GBP listing
```
**0-3 points:** decent profile, harder pitch. **4-7:** real opportunity. **8+:** easy pitch — lead with this one.

---

## Prompt (paste your manual findings in)

```
Draft a cold email to a local business owner about their Google Business Profile.
I audited their profile manually and found these specific, real issues — use only
what I list below, don't invent additional problems:

Business name: [NAME]
Niche: [NICHE]
City: [CITY]
Specific issues found (list only what's real):
- [e.g., "Last 8 reviews have no owner reply"]
- [e.g., "No post since March"]
- [e.g., "Listed under 'Contractor' instead of 'Emergency Plumber'"]
A real, named competitor who's beating them and how: [e.g., "ABC Plumbing ranks above
them for 'emergency plumber [city]' despite having a lower star rating"]

Rules for the email:
- Under 150 words
- Reference the SPECIFIC issues above, not generic local-SEO language
- One clear call to action: a free 15-minute audit call, no pitch language
- No em dashes
- Close with a concrete choice ("Would Tuesday or Wednesday work?"), not an open
  "let me know if you're interested"
- Sound like someone who looked at their profile and is pointing out what they saw,
  not someone running an email campaign
```

## Realistic response-rate expectations (confirmed against cold-email benchmark data, not the local-SEO niche specifically)

Generic cold email: ~1-3% response. Personalized, with a real specific problem named: 5-10%, up to ~18% for highly personalized outreach (instantly.ai 2026 benchmark data). **Do not expect to close a client in 60-100 emails** — that number in early drafts of this plan was too optimistic. Real agency-growth benchmarks suggest 300-1,500 emails sent is a more realistic range for a first client, depending heavily on list quality and niche saturation. Budget your first-month expectations accordingly: send in batches, track response rate, and if 50+ well-targeted emails produce zero replies, the problem is the list or the pitch, not bad luck — change one variable (subject line, niche, or city) and test again.

---

## What never gets automated here

The audit (steps above) can be Claude-assisted for drafting. **Sending the email is always a human action.** Batch-sending automated cold email is spam, burns your sending domain's reputation, and a personalization error at scale (wrong name, wrong niche) destroys credibility in one shot.
