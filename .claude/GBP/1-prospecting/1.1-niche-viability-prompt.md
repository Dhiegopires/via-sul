# Step 1.1 — Niche & City Viability
> AI prompt. Run in a new chat before spending time prospecting a niche/city combo. Output: a go/no-go plus what to expect.

---

## Why this runs first

Not every niche/city combo is worth the hours it takes to audit 10 profiles and write cold emails. This filters before you spend that time, not after.

---

## Confirmed niche tiers (Whitespark, BrightLocal, Synup, Blueprint Training — see root README's "what changed" section for what was and wasn't independently verified)

**Tier 1 — start here:** plumbers, electricians, HVAC, roofing, handyman services. Urgent search intent, owner has no time to self-manage marketing, typically under-optimized profiles. Confirmed job-ticket ranges: roofing $10,000–25,000+ per job (Hook Agency), HVAC full replacement $5,000–15,000 (industry-standard range, not tied to a single named source — verify current local pricing before quoting this to a client).

**Tier 2 — after your first few clients:** dentists, chiropractors, med spas, personal injury lawyers. Higher ticket per customer, but longer sales cycles and pickier clients. Personal-injury local SEO spend runs a wide real range — solo attorneys around $1,500–4,000/mo, competitive practices $5,000–20,000/mo — don't anchor a pitch to a single number in this niche without checking current local competition.

**Avoid at the start:** restaurants (high owner turnover, thin margins, heavier negative-review management).

---

## Prompt

```
I'm evaluating whether [NICHE] in [CITY, STATE] is worth prospecting for Google
Business Profile management.

Given what's confirmed about this niche tier (urgency of search intent, typical
job ticket size, how commonly profiles in this space are poorly optimized),
help me think through:

1. Is this a Tier 1 or Tier 2 niche by the criteria above, and does that match
   what I should expect for sales-cycle length and effort?
2. What are 3-5 specific search terms a customer in this niche would actually type
   into Google Maps in this city? (not generic — "emergency electrician [city]"
   not just "electrician")
3. What red flags would tell me this specific city/niche combo is already saturated
   with well-optimized competitors (and therefore not a good use of my next few hours)?

Niche: [NICHE]
City: [CITY, STATE]
```

---

## What to do with the output

Move to `1.2-prospect-audit-prompt.md` only if the niche/city combo passes this check. If it's saturated, try a different city in the same niche before abandoning the niche entirely — city-level competition varies more than niche-level demand.
