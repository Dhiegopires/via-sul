# Step 3.2 — Review Response Drafting
> AI prompt. Claude drafts, a human always personalizes and sends. Negative reviews especially — never let a drafted response go out unread.

---

## Why response matters (confirmed, Whitespark 2026)

Whether and how fast a business responds to reviews is itself a ranking signal (engagement), separate from the star rating. Reviews are ~20% of Map Pack ranking weight and rising. Respond within 48 hours, every time.

---

## Prompt

```
Draft responses to the following Google reviews for [BUSINESS NAME], a [NICHE]
in [CITY].

For each response:
- Reference something SPECIFIC from the review (the service mentioned, a detail
  they gave) — never a generic template reply
- Work in natural keywords where they fit without forcing: [e.g., "plumbing
  service", "drain repair", "Austin"]
- Positive reviews: thank them + invite them back. Maximum 3 sentences.
- Negative reviews: empathy first, then a concrete next step, then move the
  conversation offline (phone/email). Maximum 4 sentences. Never defensive,
  never argue the facts in public.
- No two responses should read like the same template with names swapped

Reviews:
[paste the actual review text for each, one at a time]
```

---

## Human review before sending — non-negotiable for negative reviews

- [ ] Does the response actually address what the reviewer said, or is it generic enough to paste anywhere?
- [ ] For negative reviews: does it avoid arguing, blaming the customer, or revealing anything confidential about the job/customer?
- [ ] Would the business owner be comfortable with a stranger reading this response and judging the business by it?

If a negative review is clearly fake, spam, or from a competitor: don't respond publicly at all — report it through Google's official review-flagging channel instead (support.google.com/business), and let the client know it's been reported.
