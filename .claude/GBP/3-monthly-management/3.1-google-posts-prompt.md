# Step 3.1 — Weekly Google Posts (Batch Generation)
> AI prompt. Run once a month to draft the whole month's posts at once. A human reviews and schedules each one — never auto-published.

---

## Why this happens every week regardless of ranking impact

**Confirmed: Posts do not move ranking directly.** Sterling Sky ran a real 9-week test across 441 keywords (sterlingsky.ca/do-google-posts-impact-ranking/) and found no ranking effect from posting frequency. Post anyway, because: it's one of the cheapest things to automate with Claude, it keeps the profile looking active to a human visitor deciding whether to call, and it's believed to feed Google's AI-generated Maps summaries in 2026 (plausible, not independently confirmed — treat as a bonus reason, not the main one).

---

## Prompt

```
Create 4 Google Posts for [BUSINESS: e.g., "emergency plumber in Austin, TX"].

Tone: professional but approachable, not corporate.

Each post needs:
- Body text: 150-200 words
- Natural keywords worked in: [e.g., "plumber Austin", "emergency plumbing",
  "drain cleaning"] — don't force all of them into every post
- A call-to-action button type: [Call Now / Book Online / Learn More]

Make one of each type:
1. What's New (expires in 7 days) — a recent update, completed job, or seasonal
   relevance
2. Offer (needs a code or specific deal) — [fill in the actual offer, or skip
   this type if there's no real offer this month]
3. Event (runs until a specific date) — [fill in if applicable, or skip]
4. Update — a general credibility/trust post (certification, years in business,
   a real number)

Real details to work from (don't invent facts not listed here):
[Paste: recent jobs completed, any real promotions, credentials, years in
business, actual service area]

Rules: no emoji overuse, nothing that reads like generic marketing copy,
every post must contain at least one specific, real detail from what I gave you above.
```

---

## Human review before scheduling

- [ ] Every claim in the draft traces to something real you provided — nothing invented
- [ ] No two posts this month repeat the same claim or number
- [ ] Offer/Event posts have real, current details (a stale coupon code is worse than no offer post)
