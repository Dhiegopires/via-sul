# User Journeys: AI Execution Prompt

You are generating a user journey document for a VellumWire client. The output is a structured `.md` file saved in the client project. Your task: map realistic, specific user journeys for each persona based on the inputs below.

Journeys must reflect how real people actually behave, not textbook examples. A real user skips pages, gets distracted, comes back, and converts on the third visit. Model that.

## WHAT TO PRODUCE

For each persona (minimum 2, maximum 4), map a complete journey with these stages:

**Stage 1: Trigger**
What problem or event caused them to start searching? What exact words did they type into Google? What emotional state are they in?

**Stage 2: First contact**
Which page do they land on from search? What do they see first? What makes them stay or bounce?

**Stage 3: Exploration**
Which pages do they visit next and in what order? What questions are they asking at each step? What would make them leave?

**Stage 4: Objection**
What doubt or hesitation slows them down before converting? How does the site address it?

**Stage 5: Conversion**
Which page and which CTA do they convert on? What does the conversion action look like?

**Stage 6: Drop-off risk**
At which point in the journey is this persona most likely to abandon? What would prevent it?

Also produce for each persona:
- A one-paragraph profile: who they are, what they care about, what they don't have time for
- The single most important thing the site must communicate to this persona in the first 5 seconds
- The single biggest mistake the site could make that would lose this persona

---

## OUTPUT FORMAT

Gere um arquivo `.md` salvo como `user-journeys-[cliente].md` na pasta do projeto. Estrutura:

1. **Cabeçalho**: nome do cliente, data, número de personas
2. **Uma seção por persona**: nome, perfil em 2–3 linhas, callouts (motivação principal, maior obstáculo, critério de decisão)
3. **Jornada por etapa**: Awareness → Consideração → Comparação → Decisão → Pós-conversão — para cada etapa: onde a persona está, o que busca, qual página do site intercepta, ponto de atrito, gatilho de avanço
4. **Risco de drop-off**: onde e por quê a persona abandona o funil
5. **Momento de conversão**: CTA específico, página, ação que ocorre

Para dados ausentes: `> ⚠️ [DATA NEEDED: descreva o que falta]`

## TONE RULES

- Journeys must feel like real people, not archetypes
- Use specific language: "she types 'how to reduce employee turnover'" not "the user searches for the product"
- Name friction points directly: "the pricing page has no anchor price, so she leaves"
- Conversion moments must be specific: which CTA, which page, what happens next
- No AI language anywhere in the document

---

## CRITICAL AI GUARDRAILS (HALLUCINATION-PROOFING)

To prevent AI hallucinations and generic "slop" output, you must strictly follow these rules:
1. **Sitemap Grounding**: Do not reference or invent any page that is not in the provided approved sitemap. All user navigation steps must map only to approved URLs from the sitemap.
2. **Objection Reality**: Objections and friction points must be derived directly from the intake form personas. Do not invent fictitious doubts that are unrelated to the client's actual niche.
3. **No AI Copy Patterns**:
   - Do not use em dashes (`—`) in copy.
   - Do not use emojis in written body text or headlines.
   - Do not use SaaS clichés ("Not only X, but Y", "Transform your business").
