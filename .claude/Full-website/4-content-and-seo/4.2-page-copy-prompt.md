## TONE RULES

Apply these rules to every word written. They are not suggestions.

- **Direct:** name the problem without softening it. "Your site is losing leads" not "there may be room for improvement in conversion."
- **Specific:** every claim must have a number, a name, or a concrete outcome. "Reduces turnover by 40%" not "helps reduce turnover."
- **Confident:** state the benefit as fact. "You'll know who's at risk before they resign" not "potentially helping you identify at-risk employees."
- **Results-framed:** lead with outcome, follow with mechanism. "Cut onboarding time in half, automated from day one" not "our automated onboarding system can help reduce time."
- **No AI language:** never write "crucial", "leverage", "holistic", "seamless", "cutting-edge", "innovative", "synergy", "ecosystem", "empower", "robust", "scalable solution", "in today's landscape", "it is worth noting", "game-changer."
- **No throat-clearing:** never start a headline with "Discover", "Unlock", "Transform", "Revolutionize", or "Welcome to."
- **CTA specificity:** every CTA must state exactly what happens next. "Book a 15-min call" not "Get in touch." "See it live in 30 minutes" not "Request a demo."
- **CTA honesty:** never write a CTA or alternative-link label that promises a destination the site doesn't actually have yet. If you write "prefer to send a message? Access the contact page," a real contact page (or lean capture form) must exist and be the actual href — do not write copy that assumes a destination will be built later, and never let it quietly point to the same place as the CTA it's supposed to be an alternative to. If the destination doesn't exist yet, flag `[LINK NEEDED: describe the destination this copy promises]` instead of writing the sentence as if it does.

## WHAT TO WRITE

For every page in the sitemap, produce all of the following. Follow the H1 and H2s exactly as defined in the approved SEO structure. Do not change them.

**For every page:**
- Eyebrow label (above H1): 2–4 words, category or context, not a headline
- H1: use exactly the approved H1 from the SEO structure
- Hero subtitle: 2–3 lines, max 120 characters, expands on the H1, speaks directly to primary stakeholder
- Primary CTA text: specific action, max 5 words. If Step 2.3 says this page's awareness level requires agitation before the primary ask, the primary CTA text goes in the section 2.3 names, not the hero — write the hero's CTA as the lower-commitment secondary action instead, per 2.3's rule
- Secondary CTA text: lower commitment, max 4 words
- Trust line below CTAs: removes the biggest objection in one sentence

**For every H2 section:**
- H2: use exactly the approved H2 from the SEO structure
- Section subtitle: 1–2 lines expanding on the H2
- Body copy or feature list: depending on section type
- The copy must do the job of this section's formula stage and conversion lever, as assigned in Step 2.3 — an "Agitate" section must intensify the specific pain named in the persona's Trigger (Step 2.2), not restate the problem neutrally. A "Guide" section must establish authority/trust, not list features. A section labeled with the anxiety lever must directly name and defuse the objection from Step 2.2's Drop-off risk, not general reassurance. Write to the assigned job, not to what sounds good in isolation.

**For feature/module descriptions:**
- Feature name
- One-line benefit statement (not a feature description, a benefit)
- 2–3 line body expanding the benefit with specific detail

**For social proof sections:**
- Logos bar label: one line above client logos
- Metrics row: number + label + one-line explanation for each metric (use only real numbers from client data)
- Testimonial: if client provided quotes, format them. If not, write [TESTIMONIAL NEEDED: specify what result to highlight]

**For FAQ sections:**
- Minimum 6 questions written as a real potential client would ask them, not as a company would phrase them
- Answers: direct, 3–5 lines, no hedging

**For CTA final sections:**
- Headline: creates urgency or addresses the final objection
- Subtitle: 1–2 lines
- Primary CTA
- Secondary link text

## CRITICAL AI GUARDRAILS (HALLUCINATION-PROOFING)

To prevent AI hallucinations and generic "slop" output, you must strictly follow these rules:
1. **Zero Hallucination of Credentials/Data**: Do not invent, estimate, or approximate testimonials, statistics, customer numbers, awards, or history. If the inputs do not provide these, you must output `[DATA NEEDED: describe what's missing]` styled in danger color.
2. **Copy Verification table**: Make sure the generated copy audit table at the end flags any CTA containing generic verbs ("contact", "learn more", "get started") as a WARNING.
3. **No AI Copy Patterns**:
   - Do not use em dashes (`—`) in copy.
   - Do not use emojis in written body text or headlines.
   - Do not use SaaS clichés ("Not only X, but Y", "Transform your business").
   - Do not use banned throat-clearing/filler words listed in the tone rules.
