# Client Discovery Form
> Send to the client as soon as the project is signed. Request a response within 48h.
> Instructions for the client: "The more specific you are, the better the result. Avoid generic answers. They produce generic websites."

---

## How to use this file

1. Copy the questions below and paste them into a Google Form, Notion page, or email
2. Adapt the tone to the client's profile (more formal or more casual)
3. Once you receive the answers, paste everything into the Architecture prompt and Copy prompt
4. Vague answers = send the question back before moving forward

---

## Block 1: The Business

**1.1 Describe what your company does in one sentence.**
*(Not what it is, but what it does and for whom. E.g. "We help clinics schedule appointments without a receptionist.")*

**1.2 What is the main problem you solve for your client?**

**1.3 What is the concrete result your client gets after using your product or service?**
*(Numbers, time saved, money generated or saved, problem eliminated)*

**1.4 How long has the company existed? What is its current size?**
*(Employees, active clients, approximate revenue: share whatever you can)*

**1.5 Which cities, states, or countries do you operate in?**

**1.6 Where do your clients/leads come from today? Break it down by channel with rough numbers or percentages if you have them.**
*(Referral, existing website, events/conferences, cold outreach, paid ads, social media, WhatsApp, other. Be specific: "about 15 clients/month, roughly 70% referral, 20% events, 10% website" is useful. "Mixed sources" is not.)*

**1.7 Has anyone ever paid you for this exact product or service? How do you know people want it?**
*(Real answer, not aspirational: number of paying clients, retention, repeat business, waitlist, whatever proves demand already exists — separate from anything the website will do.)*

---

## Block 2: The Product or Service

**2.1 List all your products or services.**
*(Name + one-line description for each)*

**2.2 Which one is most important for the website to communicate?**

**2.3 What is your biggest differentiator from competitors?**
*(Be specific. "Better customer service" doesn't count.)*

**2.4 Do you have any proprietary technology, process, or methodology?**

**2.5 Do you have data or metrics that prove your results?**
*(Examples: "we reduced turnover by 40%", "500 companies served", "92% satisfaction rate")*

---

## Block 3: The Ideal Client

**3.1 Describe your ideal client in as much detail as possible.**
*(Job title, industry, company size, revenue range, location)*

**3.2 Who makes the buying decision? Is it the same person who uses the product?**

**3.3 What are the main doubts or objections your client has before buying?**

**3.4 What makes your client choose a competitor instead of you?**

**3.5 Describe the best client you've ever had. What made them ideal?**

---

## Block 4: The Website

**4.1 What is the primary goal of the website?**
*(Generate leads, sell directly, present the company, recruit talent, other)*

**4.2 What action do you want the visitor to take when they land on the site?**
*(Be specific: "fill out a demo request form", "call us", "buy through checkout")*

**4.3 Do you have a current website? If so, what is its biggest problem in your view?**

**4.4 Share 2–3 websites from any industry that you admire aesthetically.**
*(Please share links)*

**4.5 Share 1 website that you definitely do NOT want yours to look like.**
*(And why)*

**4.6 Are there any visual elements you want to keep? (colors, logo, fonts)**

**4.7 Are there any words, phrases, or approaches you do NOT want on the site?**
*(E.g. "don't use the word innovative", "no handshake stock photos")*

---

## Block 5: Competition and Market

**5.1 Name your 3 main direct competitors.**
*(Name + website)*

**5.2 What do they do better than you today?**

**5.3 What do you do better than them?**

**5.4 How do your clients typically find you today?**
*(Referral, Google, LinkedIn, events, other)*

**5.5 Are there any regulations, certifications, or compliance requirements in your industry that the site needs to mention?**

---

## Block 6: Content and Social Proof

**6.1 Do you have client testimonials I can use? If so, please share them.**

**6.2 Do you have documented case studies with results?**
*(Before and after, metrics, client story)*

**6.3 Do you have client logos I can display on the site?**

**6.4 Do you have certifications, awards, press coverage, or relevant partnerships?**

**6.5 Do you have photos or videos of the product/service in use that I can use?**

---

## Block 7: Voice of the Customer

> This block exists because everything above is the client's view of their own business. It is not a substitute for hearing from real customers. Send this block even if it feels like extra friction — a generic site is what happens when every input comes from the business side only.

**7.1 Forward any raw customer feedback you already have, unedited.**
*(Support tickets, app/Google reviews, NPS or CSAT comments, sales call notes, exit-survey or churn responses — whatever exists. Don't summarize it, forward it as-is. Summarizing strips the specific language real customers use, which is exactly what's useful.)*

**7.2 If you don't have documented feedback, can you forward this to 3–5 real customers?**

> *Three quick questions, two minutes:*
> 1. What almost stopped you from buying / signing up?
> 2. What's the one thing this product/service actually changed for you?
> 3. If you described us to a colleague in one sentence, what would you say?

Send the answers back as-is, in the customer's own words.

**7.3 If neither 7.1 nor 7.2 is possible right now, say so explicitly.**
*(This gets flagged as `[DATA NEEDED: no direct customer input available]` and carried forward — it does not block the project, but it means every claim about what customers want is coming from you, not from them. Better to know that going in than to discover it after launch.)*

---

## Block 8: Technical and Timeline

**8.1 Do you have a registered domain? Which one?**

**8.2 Do you have current hosting? Where?**

**8.3 Are there any integrations required?**
*(CRM, form, WhatsApp, chat, booking system, other)*

**8.4 Does the site need a blog or content area?**

**8.5 What is your deadline for the site to go live?**

**8.6 Who at your company will approve the site before it goes live?**
*(Name and title. Avoid surprises at the end.)*

**8.7 Do you already have a privacy policy (política de privacidade)? If yes, share it. If no, who handles data collected through the site (name, email, phone from forms or WhatsApp) — do you have a CRM or spreadsheet, and who has access?**
*(Any site that collects personal data in Brazil needs an LGPD-compliant privacy policy page and, if cookies/analytics are used, a cookie consent banner. This is not optional legal decoration — flag it now so Step 2's sitemap includes the privacy policy page and Step 5 wires the consent banner and a consent checkbox on every form, instead of discovering the gap at Step 6.)*

---

## Post-receipt checklist

Before moving to step 2, confirm:

- [ ] All questions answered, no blank fields
- [ ] No generic answers (if any, return to client before proceeding)
- [ ] Real data collected: metrics, client names, specific differentiators
- [ ] Reference sites and anti-reference sites received
- [ ] Deadline and approvers confirmed
- [ ] Voice-of-customer received (raw feedback, outreach answers, or explicit `[DATA NEEDED]` flag — not silently skipped)
- [ ] Validation meeting scheduled
