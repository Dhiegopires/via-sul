# Lead Generation Viability Check: AI Execution Prompt

You are assessing whether a website redesign can realistically be expected to move the needle on lead generation for this client, before VellumWire commits to selling "we generate leads" as part of this engagement. This is not a judgment of the client as a business. It is a specific, narrow question: **is the reason this client isn't getting leads from their website a website problem, or a demand problem?** A website fixes the first. Nothing about a website fixes the second.

## WHAT TO PRODUCE

### Part 1: Where does demand evidence actually come from

State plainly, from 1.6 and 1.7's answers: does this business have real, paying, repeat, or referred customers for this exact offer, through any channel? List the channels and the evidence for each — a number, a name, a retention rate, anything concrete. If the client's answer was vague ("we have some clients"), say so explicitly — do not fill the gap with an assumption of demand that wasn't stated.

### Part 2: Classify — GREEN / YELLOW / RED

**GREEN — proceed with confidence. This is the profile a conditional lead-generation guarantee (see `0-briefing-master/lead-guarantee-terms.md`) is built for.**
The client has real, demonstrated demand for this exact offer through at least one channel that is not the current website (referral, events, repeat business, outbound, WhatsApp, whatever). The website specifically is the underperforming channel relative to the others. This means the problem being solved is proven; the site's structure is the variable in question — exactly the kind of case this methodology's conversion-architecture work (Step 2.3 onward) is built to fix.

**YELLOW — proceed, but do not offer the conditional guarantee, and say so to the client explicitly before starting.**
Some demand signal exists but it's thin: a new offer (under ~6 months old), very few clients so far, no channel has produced volume yet, or the price point has never actually been tested against a real buyer. The website may be the first real test this offer gets. Structural best practice still applies (mobile-first, CTA clarity, honest links, all of it) — but there's no prior channel to prove the demand exists, so there's nothing to point to if the site doesn't convert either. Flag this distinction to the client in plain language: "we're building the best possible version of this page, but this is also the first real market test of the offer itself."

**RED — flag to Dhiego before selling anything framed around lead generation. Do not silently proceed.**
No evidence anyone has ever paid for this exact offer through any channel, ever — or the client has been trying to sell this specific thing for 12+ months with zero traction anywhere, on or off the web. In this case, recommend the smaller `Website-checkup` engagement or an honest conversation about the offer itself before scoping a Full Web Rebuild sold on lead generation. Selling "we generate leads" against an unproven offer sets VellumWire up to absorb blame for a demand problem it did not create and cannot fix with page structure.

### Part 3: What this classification changes downstream

State explicitly:
- Whether `0-briefing-master/lead-guarantee-terms.md` should be offered to this client (GREEN only)
- What to tell the client about expectations, in one direct sentence they'll actually remember
- Whether the site audit (1.2) or market research (1.3) already surfaced anything that contradicts the client's own answers to 1.6/1.7 — if the client claims strong demand but the market research found no evidence of them anywhere in the competitive landscape, or the site audit found the business has no reviews/presence anywhere, flag the contradiction rather than resolving it silently in the client's favor.

## TONE RULES

- This is an internal document. Direct, not diplomatic. If the honest read is RED, write RED — do not soften it into YELLOW to avoid an uncomfortable conversation.
- Never invent demand evidence the client didn't actually state. "They probably have some clients" is not evidence.
- The classification is about the offer's proven demand, not about whether the client is likeable, hardworking, or deserving. Keep it to the narrow question.

---

## CRITICAL AI GUARDRAILS (HALLUCINATION-PROOFING)

1. **No Invented Evidence**: Every piece of demand evidence cited must trace to the client's literal answer in 1.6/1.7, or to a finding in 1.2/1.3. Do not infer demand from company size, industry reputation, or how professional the client sounds.
2. **No Silent Downgrade of RED to YELLOW**: If the evidence genuinely shows zero proof of demand anywhere, classify RED even if that's an uncomfortable business conversation. This document exists specifically to have that conversation before the money and the promise are made, not after.
3. **No AI Copy Patterns**:
   - Do not use em dashes (`—`) in copy.
   - Do not use emojis in written body text or headlines.
   - Do not use SaaS clichés ("Not only X, but Y", "Transform your business").
